I.T. Support/Support Specialist

An I.T. Support Specialist assist computer users and organizations. This position are responsible for analyzing, troubleshooting and evaluating technology issues. This role may support internal clients, helping to keep employees productive, or external customers of a company, helping them via the telephone or a ticketing system. This role is sometimes called a Help Desk Technician/Support because you deal with external customers of a company via a ticketing system.

  • Education

    While employers do prefer a degree an associates and bachelors degree, it is generally not required and having general computer knowledge is more valued. I.T. degrees or any Computer related degrees can be applied to this position. Having a certification or experience related to the job can also massively help land you a position.

  • Technical Skills

    Troubleshooting and general knowledge of how a computer works is the most important aspect in this job. Learning about the Operating System, and Hardware/Software Architecture will help you understand how to help your organization with external and internal issues. Learning how a ticketing system works is also recommended, but different companies do use different systems.

  • Soft Skills

    You will be dealing with external customers as well as internal customers in this position. Customer service skills is valued as well as communication skills. Problem-solving skills will be valued when dealing with technological issues and troubleshooting.

More on I.T. Support

An I.T. Support position is considered one of the entry points into the I.T. industry. Most I.T. related majors start their careers as an I.T. Support or a Help Desk. They go on to more specialized fields such as System Admin, Network specialists, Security Specialists and more.

Recommended Certifications

The industry standard certification for an I.T. Support is the CompTIA A+ certification. You will need to pass two core exams in order to get this certification. Studying for this exam will get you acquinted and knowledgeable on all skills needed to being an I.T. Support from hardware to security.

Salary

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary of an I.T. Support position or other similar job titles is $49,770. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $34,220, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $91,060.

Common Job Duties

  • Analyze customers' computer problem to diagnose it and determine the cause
  • Document customers' descriptions of their computer problems
  • Set up or repair computer equipment and related devices
  • Install and train users on new hardware or software

System and Network Administrator

A system and network adminstrator or a sysadmin, are important in large-scale organizations with computer systems. This position oversees the network system and the computer system of the organization. From day to day operations, to hundreds of computers, or even data communication systems, a sysadmin manages the servers and equipments.

  • Education

    Post-secondary education is recommended when aiming for a sysadmin position. A degree related to Computer/Information Technologies are required and in some cases, a master's degree is required. Keeping up with new technology trends is valued since you deal with all aspect of technology in your organization.

  • Technical Skills

    Technical skills are similar to the I.T. Support role but taken a step further. Mastery of operating systems, hardware/software architecture and general technology will be required. Troubleshooting issues is also required. Network systems and security systems knowledge is also needed since you are dealing with every aspect of the computer system.

  • Soft Skills

    You will be dealing more with internal employees to solve any technological related issues. Training employees and making documents will be common in this position. Communication skills and listening skills is very much valued. Problem-solving skills is also required when troubleshooting. You must have good judgement and good evaluation skills in order to efficiently analyze computer systems. Having good multi-tasking ability is valued since there is alot of expectations on you.

More on System Administration

A sysadmin is a position that leans towards the mid-career of a technology major. In most organizations, a sysadmin is what follows after an I.T. Support. There are also cases where you can be a sysadmin right away due to having mastery of technical skills or obtaining certifications.

Recommended Certifications

Companies generally require their network and computer systems administrators to be certified in the products they use. Certification programs usually are offered directly from vendors or from vendor-neutral certification providers. If a Microsoft environment is used, the Microsoft Azure Solutions Architect Expert certificate is recommended. Industry standard certifications like the CompTIA A+ is also recommended. The CompTIA Network+ and Security+ certifications is also valued when dealing with large-scale operations.

Salary

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary of a System Admin or other similar job titles is $80,600. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $49,560, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $130,830.

Common Job Duties

  • Determine an organization's system needs and install network hardware and software
  • Maintain network and computer system security
  • Evaluate and optimize network or system performance
  • Add users to a network, and assign and update security permissions on the network
  • Train users in the proper use of hardware and software

Network Specialist/Architect

A Computer network architect/specialist is asked to design,build, and maintain data communication networks, including local area networks (LANs), wide area networks (WANs), and Intranets. This position deals with the network of a computer system in an organization. Whether it be small connections from building to building or implementing a cloud network, you will be dealing with every aspect of a network system. You will also maintain and uphold security standards for the network.

  • Education

    Post-secondary education is recommended. A bachelors in a computer/information degeree is required while some employers do look for a masters degree. Since this is a specialized field, any knowledge of network systems specifically is highly valued. That goes for any specialized certificates, classes, programs and bootcamps.

  • Technical Skills

    Network specialist/architect is a specialized field so having mastery over network systems and data communication is required. You will be dealing with organization's network from implementation to maintaining it so you will be required to learn about physically making a plan for how to build/design your network. You must also be able to know how network systems work from theory to real-life applications of how it actually works. Data communications and local area network topics including communications theory, communication hardware and software, TCP/IP, Internet infrastructure and standards, LANs, WANs, cloud computing and Internet security.

  • Soft Skills

    Being detail-oriented is high recommended when being in this role. You will be dealing with specific connections and testing multiple systems. Being able to evaluate your design and analyze your system is also valued. Problem-solving skills will also be valuable when maintaining technology.

More on Network Specialist/Architect

A Network Specialist/Architect is in a similar standing as a sysadmin where it is a mid-career position. You can build your way up from being an I.T. Support into this role but most Network Specialist have alot of years under their belt as a sysadmin and I.T. Support.

Recommended Certifications

Vendor-specific certification verifies a set of skills to ensure network architects are able to work in specific networking environments. Organizations require the network specialist/architect to be certified in the products/systems they use. Industry standard network certifications like Network+and CCNA will be highly valued.

Salary

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary of a Network Specialist position or other similar job titles is $120,520. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $63,240, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $168,890.

Common Job Duties

  • Create plans and layouts for data communication networks
  • Consider information security when designing networks
  • Upgrade hardware, such as routers or adaptors, and software, such as network drivers, as needed to support computer networks
  • Research new networking technologies to determine what would best support their organization in the future
  • Maintain network systems

Information Security Analyst

Information Security Analyst deals with creating and carrying out security measures to protect an organizations computer systems. This position is similar to a Network Specialist where it is a very specialized position in an organization. Your responsiblity and value in this position is high due to the fact you might be protecting the organization's confidential and sensitive data.

  • Education

    A bachelor's degree in a computer/information related field is highly valued. But this position relies more on industry training and certifications due to the role being so specialized.

  • Technical Skills

    It is required for Information Security Analysts to know about cyber security and protecting the security of different types of technology. You must be able to set up firewalls and encryption programs while making a plan to evaluate the system for vulnerabilities. You must be able to create protocols for preventing a breach, during a breach and what to do after a breach. You also must be knowledgeable about security products and implementing those services towards a fleet of computers.

  • Soft Skills

    Similar to the Network Specialist role, you must possess analytical skills in order to determine risks in the system. It is not often brought up but having great creativity is essential to thinking of new ways to protect the system. You also must teach and document procedures to employees so a strong communication skill is required. Working with technology you must be detail-oriented and have strong problem-solving skills to troubleshoot.

More on Information Security Analyst

Similar to the Network Specialist, this position needs work experience in a similar and related occupation like Sysadmin. It is not common for this position to be an entry type role but it is possible if you have training and certifications under your belt. Auditing is also a sub-specialization under information security.

Recommended Certifications

In the security field, employers prefor those who have certifications. The Security+ certification is highly needed because it is an industry standard when it comes to information security and cybersecurity. The CISSP certification is valued for expert/higher level positions in this field.

Salary

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary of an Information Security Analyst position or other similar job titles is $102,600. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $61,520, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $165,920.

Common Job Duties

  • Monitor their organization's networks for security breaches and investigate when one occurs
  • Use and maintain software, such as firewalls and data encryption programs, to protect sensitive information
  • Prepare reports that document general metrics, attempted attacks, and security breaches
  • Develop security standards and best practices for their organization
  • Help computer users when they need to install or learn about new security products and procedures

Website Designer/Developer

A website developer creates and maintains websites. They also deal with performance and scalability of the websites. Some web developers even create content for the sites they create. While a developer deals with the technical aspect of the website, a website designer focuses on how the website looks and how responsive it is.

  • Education

    A web developer/designers requirements is very leniant compared to other occupations in this field. A high school diploma can cut it but a degree will be highly valued. Employers who hire in this position prefer those who truly have the skills and knowledge to create and manage websites. Work experience, projects, and most importantly a portfolio that can show your ability will be more effective than just showing educational credentials.

  • Technical Skills

    There are multiple sub-positions in this career, a front-end developer deals with how the website looks and its layout. HTML,CSS, and JavaScript is required. A back end developer deals with the functionality and interactivity of the website. Object oriented languages like Python and Java will be required. Database languages like SQL and NoSQL will also be valued to deal with the data aspect of the website. A person who works in both ends is called a Full Stack Developer.

  • Soft Skills

    You will be working on specific projects as a website developer/designer. Being able to listen to what the client or your boss wants for the website is important. Also being able to communicate back issues or concerns is important as well. Being detail-oriented and creative will help you with the technical side of developing and designing websites. As with anyone who deals with code, you must be able to problem solve issues or bugs in your code.

More on Website Developer/Designer

Similar to the Network Specialist, this position needs work experience in a similar and related occupation like Sysadmin. It is not common for this position to be an entry type role but it is possible if you have training and certifications under your belt. Auditing is also a sub-specialization under information security.

Portfolios

Certifications do not really yield a high value when it comes to Website development and designing. Employers often ask for examples of work you have done whether it be personal websites, work related websites or even school project related websites. Most often create an online portfolio that can show off your creativity and all your projects in one site.

Salary

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary of a Website Designer/Developer position or other similar job titles is $79,890. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $38,550, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $129,760..

Common Job Duties

  • Meet with clients or management to discuss the needs, design, and functionality of a website or interface
  • Create and test applications, interfaces, and navigation menus for a website
  • Write code for the website, using programming languages such as HTML
  • Integrate graphics, audio, and video into the website
  • Design and develop graphics
  • Monitor website traffic

Database Developer/Adminstrator

A database developer/adminstrator creates, manages and organizes databases, a system to store all types of data. They make it easier for others to look up information and also to help an organization store their data in an organized way. They must keep the system in check, secure the system at all times, and grant authorization to all users depending on the situation.

  • Education

    A bachelor's degree in the computer field is typically required. Some employers even prefer having a history or background in business along with I.T. A master's degree with a specific focus on managing data or databases is also highly valued since knowing how to use SQL is a very specialized position.

  • Technical Skills

    The main aspect of being a database developer is knowing how to interact with a database. SQL is a common way to query data but there are multiple other languages out there that do the same.

  • Soft Skills

    Being able to analyze data is essential to being a good database developer. Working with code requires one to be detail-oriented and solve problems such as bugs in your code.

More on Database Developer/Admistrator

There are System DBAs and Application DBAs which are sub positions in a database developer's career. System DBAs deal with the infastracture and technical side of a database while Application DBAs deal with creating and managing applications for external and internal customers to use.

Programming Language

SQL is the industry standard for database development that is known for its scalability and robustness. There are many other query languages out there but SQL is the most used. Learning Python and R is always valued when working in a field of data as those general purpose languages can be used for database management, system management and integration.

Salary

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary of a DBA or other similar job titles is $96,710. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $63,260, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $169,500..

Common Job Duties

  • Identify user needs to create and administer databases
  • Design and build new databases
  • Ensure that databases operate efficiently and without error
  • Make and test modifications to database structure when needed
  • Maintain databases and update permissions

Software Developer/Engineer

Software developers create software to meet user needs by employing diagrams and models, writing code, and ensuring overall functionality. These professionals design, build, and implement computer programs and applications.

  • Education

    A bachelor's degree in the computer science or in a related field is typically required. However, you can still become a software developer/engineer through alternatives such as self-study and/or bootcamps.

  • Technical Skills

    Software Developers need to have a good knowledge base on different types of programming languages like Python, Java, and C# to name a few. They must know how a computer thinks, and must also have a good knowledge base of data structures and algorithms.

  • Soft Skills

    Developers must be able to solve complex problems in order to have an application software run smoothly. They must also be able to communicate with other members of the team and clients as well to ensure proper requirements when developing the application are added. They must also be able to have close attention to detail, as it is necessary to spot a problem before it causes trouble.

More on being a Software Developer/Engineer

Being a software developer is rewarding, however it takes a lot of time and dedication to become one. To become one, it is recommended to spend a good time of your day practicing code, whether by creating a project for fun or by solving problems with sites made for that such as LeetCode.

Build a Portfolio!

In order to prove you can be a software developer, you must show that you can create things with the knowledge and practice you gained overtime. This involves creating a portfolio in github that shows the projects you did. The more completed projects you have in your portfolio, the more qualified you are, as you were able to use what you know to create those projects!

Salary

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary of a software developer is $120,730. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $64,470, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $168,570..

Common Job Duties

  • Oversees software programming
  • Testing software at various phases of completion
  • Creating/Designing a layout of the software application being built
  • Building models/diagrams in order to communicate the end goal
  • Mapping out future software upgrades

Data Analyst

Data Analysts gather and interpret data in order to solve specific problems given to them by the company/client. They review data to identify key insights to a business's customers in order to solve and increase business output. They usually work with small or large datasets given to them by a business.

  • Education

    A bachelor's degree in the Computer Science, Computer/Management Information Systems, or a related field is typically required. However, you can still become a Data Analyst through a combination of alternatives such as self-study, bootcamps, and/or certifications.

  • Technical Skills

    A data analyst would need to have knowledge in data-related programming languages, such as SQL/NoSQL, Python, and Java. They must also know some data visualization tools such as Tableau, Excel, PowerBI, and MatLab.

  • Soft Skills

    Data Analysts must be able to communicate well, as they will be frequently in meetings either discussing what needs to be solved with their team members, or discussing their findings with a group of stakeholders, or the people who requsted the analyzation.

More on being a Data Analyst

Being a data analyst is considered by a lot of people to be a fun, yet long/tedious job, however, if going the no degree route, it takes a lot of time and to become one. To become one, it is recommended to spend a good time of your day practicing creating data visualiztions from open datasets you can find.

Build a Portfolio!

To show that you can be a Data Analyst, you have to be able to show that you can extract, transform, and load visualiztions and solve problems with those visualizations. This involves creating a portfolio that shows visualizations you did and what answers or findings you discovered. Almost all of the tools needed to start in data analytics are free, and you can find numerous datasets on sites like Kaggle, or you can even make your own dataset!

Salary

According to Salary.com, the median annual salary of a data analyst in the United States is $82,575, but the salary range falls in-between $73,909 and $92,384. Keep in mind this depends on a lot of factors, such as the education level, certifications, any added skills, experience level, even the location.

Common Job Duties

  • Extracting large or small sets of data from primary and/or secondary sources
  • Cleaning and preping a dataset prior to analyzing it
  • Filter data by reviewing reports given by clients or customers
  • Use statistical tools to identify, analyze, and interpret patterns and trends in data sets for diagnosis of a problem and prediction
  • Prepare reports stating trends, pattern, and predictions for business acumen to use into solving problems.

Data Scientist

Data Scientists try to discover new ways in capturing and analyzing data to be used later by the data analysts. They do this by spending time on developing models and preparing/creating algorithms. They are in charge of deploying that model by allowance and demand of an organiztion.

  • Education

    A Master's degree in Computer Science, Computer/Management Information Systems, Data Science, or a related field is typically required. What field you got your bachelor's degree does not matter, but a bachelor's in mathematics, statistics, or computer science is recommended.

  • Technical Skills

    A data scientist would need to have knowledge in data-related programming languages, such as SQL, Python, and Java. They must also know Machine/Deep Learning, and how to use it in creating new ways to capture data. They must have a great foundation of mathematics and statistics, and must know how to deal with Big Data.

  • Soft Skills

    Data Scientists must be able to communicate well, as they will be frequently in meetings discussing their findings with team members. They must have a strong business acumen, meaning they must know how to understand and deal with a business situation. They must be able to think outside the box and have solid, decision making skills.

More on being a Data Scientist

A data scientist uses data to understand and explain the phenomena around them, and help organizations make better decisions.

Anything I need to know prior to becoming one?

To become a data scientist, you must need a very strong background in mathematics and computer science. This is usually why most data scientists had pursue a bachelor's in mathematics, statistics, computer science or a related field, even Economics. You must have some experience working with large amounts of data. Being a data analyst first provides that experience. Since technology is ever-growing and changing, you must have the willingness to keep learning.

Salary

According to Glassdoor.com, the estimated total salary pay of a data scientist in the United States is $126,629, with the salary range falling to $103,892 per year. Keep in mind this depends on a lot of factors, such as the education level, certifications, any added skills, experience level, even the location.

Common Job Duties

  • Create algorithms and data models in order to forecast outcomes
  • Using machine learning techniques to improve the quality of data
  • Deploying multiple data tools like Python, SQL, and R for data analysis
  • Find patterns and trends in datasets to uncover insights

Cloud Engineer

Cloud Engineers are responsible for integrating and optimizing cloud computing services that allow organizations to operate with greater efficiency, security, and attention to detail. They are responsible for any technological duties associated with cloud computing, which involves design, planning, managment, maintenance, and support.

  • Education

    To be a cloud engineer, any degree is plausible, but it's highly recommended you have a bachelor's degree in Information Technology or Computer Science. However, to achieve this role you must also go through a combination of self-study, certifications, and bootcamps to show that you are qualified for this position(s). Same with those entering without a degree.

  • Technical Skills

    A cloud engineer possesses knowledge in technical skills such as Linux, a profound knowledge in databases, networks, and security. They must also possess a good understanding in web applications and APIs, virtualization, and must know some containerization tools.

  • Soft Skills

    Cloud Engineers must be able to communicate well as they will be in frequent meetings discussing what can be updated and what needs to be changed/removed. They must have good project management skills, as you they will be managing projects given to them by clients. They must have profound decision-making and problem solving skills. They should be able to adapt to situations, and lead a team when during a project.

More on being a Cloud Engineer

Cloud engineers are high in demand, and there are a shortage of people looking to fit into the role(s). This is not an entry-level position, as a "perfect" candidate must have not only the certifications, degree, and self-study, but must also have years of prior experience within the technology field. IT Support -> System/Network Admin -> Cloud seems to be the most popular route when entering the cloud field.

Anything I need to know prior to becoming one?

Cloud Engineer is actually a broad path and there are specialized paths you can get into, such as a cloud architect, cloud network engineer, cloud security engineer, cloud systems engineer, or a cloud software engineer.

Salary

According to Glassdoor.com, the estimated total pay for a Cloud Engineer is $152,633 per year. The average salary in the U.S. is $101,648. Additional pay added to the average salary is $50,984 per year.

Common Job Duties

  • Teaming up with engineer and development teams to evaluate and find cloud solutions
  • Modifying and updating existing systems
  • Designing, developing and deploying modular cloud-based systems
  • Interacting with clients, providing cloud support, and making recommendations based on client needs

DevOps Engineer

A DevOps engineer is an IT professional who has an in-depth understanding of the software development lifecycle. They need to understand why the organization had to move from one model to the other, the shortcomings of the previous model, and the benefits of the new model.

  • Education

    To be a DevOps engineer you must obtain a bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, or a related field. However, to achieve this role you must also go through a combination of self-study, certifications, and bootcamps to show that you are qualified for this position(s). You may be able to self-study into this career, but it will take a lot of time, dedication, and also years of experience to reach.

  • Technical Skills

    A DevOps engineer must have knowledge and experience with using programming languages such as Ruby, Python, and JavaScript. They must also possess knowledge and understanding of the fundamentals of the Linux Command Line Interface. They need to know Source Code Management tools such as Git, CVS, or Mercurial, and must know how to build an application.

  • Soft Skills

    Just like Cloud Engineers, DevOps Engineers must be able to communicate well as they will be in frequent meetings discussing what can be updated and what needs to be changed/removed. They must have good project management skills, as they will be managing projects given to them by clients, must have profound decision-making and problem solving skills, and should be able to adapt to situations, and lead a team during a project.

More on being a DevOps Engineer

Just like Cloud engineers, DevOps engineers are high in demand, and there are a shortage of people looking to fit into its role. This is not an entry-level position, as the "perfect" candidate must have not only the certifications, degree, and self-study, but must also have years of prior experience within the technology field.

A DevOps career has multiple career paths

Someone who is a DevOps Engineer can also get into other career paths like:

  • DevOps Architect
  • Automation Engineer
  • Security Manager
  • Software Tester

Salary

According to Glassdoor.com, the estimated total pay for a DevOps Engineer is $134,478 per year. The average salary in the U.S. is $104,906. The estimated additional pay added to the average salary is $29,572 per year. This is determined by factors such as education, years of experience, the type of projects you have done as self-study, and any certifications/specializations of vendors you fell under.

Common Job Duties

  • Write up documentation for server-side features
  • Communicate operational requirements and development forecasts with everyone in the team
  • Develop plans and processes for improvement and expansion of the current technologies being used
  • Perform routine application maintenance and troubleshooting measure to ensure the production environment runs smoothly

Business Analyst

A business analyst helps guide businesses in improving processes, products, services and software By using data to form business insights and recommend changes in those businesses and/or organizations

  • Education

    To be a Business Analyst, you must obtain a bachelor's degree in Information Technology, Computer/Management Information Systems or a business related field. You must also possess a good understanding of business acumen.

  • Technical Skills

    A business analyst has great understanding of basic Microsoft Office or Google Suite applications, such as Excel/Sheets, PowerPoint/Slides, or Word/Docs. They must possess an understanding of programming languages such as Java, Python, C++, and/or PHP. SQL and database management knowledge and understanding is required as well.

  • Soft Skills

    A business analyst must have great communication skills, as they will be the liason between the development team and the stakeholders when a project is starting. They must possess great project management and problem solving skills, as they will be constantly be working with projects within the organization. They must possess an analytical mind and must have a basic understanding of Business Intelligence.

More on being a Business Analyst

You can be a business analyst with any business related degree, but a technical degree like Management/Computer Information Systems will give you an advantage in the application and interview process.

This job is stressful

Being a business analyst is no walk in the park. Since you'll be dealing with communicating between business clients and development teams for most of your time, you will be involved in processing and analyzing huge amounts of data, unifying it, and explain your findings from that data to help the company make better business decisions. The wrong data gathered or the wrong situation analyzed may cause damage to the company

Salary

According to Salary.com, the average business analyst salary in Los Angeles is $90,935 per year. However the typical salary range falls between $82,260 to $100.890 in the U.S. overall. Keep in mind factors such as education, your skillset, and the number of years of experience all play a part in your salary.

Common Job Duties

  • Creating business analysis reports that highlights the problems and solutions
  • Driving business to make decisions based on collected data
  • Uncover areas for improvement and developing solutions to certain business processes and systems.
  • Managing projects, developing project plans, and monitoring performance

Project Manager

A Project Manager is a professional who organizes, plans, and executes projects while working within restraints like budgets and schedules. They communicate and act as the liason for the team and the business stakeholders/clients.

  • Education

    There is no specific degree required to become a Project Manager, however a degree in Management/Computer Information Systems or a business related degree is highly recommended. You must also possess a good understanding of business acumen and a solid understanding of business intelligence and data analysis. A certificate related in project management like the Project Management Professional certificate (PMP) could be really helpful.

  • Technical Skills

    A project manager must have a great understanding of basic Microsoft Office, such as Excel, PowerPoint, or Docs. An understanding of programming languages, as well as SQL and database management knowledge is highly required as well, as you will be working with multiple development teams during project building.

  • Soft Skills

    A project manager will constantly be communicating with business clients and/or team members when a project is entering a phase or is about to enter completion. They must possess great project management and problem solving skills, as they will be constantly be working with projects within the organization. They must possess an analytical mind and must have a basic understanding of business intelligence and data analysis.

More on being a Project Manager

Just like a business analyst, you can be a Project Manager with any business related degree, but a technical degree like Management/Computer Information Systems will give you an advantage in the application and interview process. However, you must have years of experience beforehand in order to become a project manager.

This job is stressful

Being a project manager is no walk in the park. You'll be dealing with multiple meetings in a day, whether it is for the project you are overseeing with the team you built/chose, or with business clients and stakeholders on what tasks and new information you have gathered or discovered. It requires a lot of communication with many people, so if you are introverted, this may not be the career you are looking for.

Salary

According to GlassDoor, the average Project Manager salary in Los Angeles is $96,776 per year. This includes additional pay, which is $6,891, which makes the average salary $89,885. Keep in mind factors such as education, your skillset, and the number of years of experience all play a part in your salary.

Common Job Duties

  • Coordinating internal resources and vendors for the flawless execution of projects
  • Creating and choosing the best team to complete a given project
  • Ensuring all projects are delivered on-time, within scope and budget
  • Perfrm risk management to minimize project risks and maintain project documentation
